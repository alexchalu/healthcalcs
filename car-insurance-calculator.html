How Car Insurance Premiums Are Calculated

Insurance companies use dozens of factors to determine your rate. The major factors include:

  • Age & Experience: Drivers under 25 and over 75 typically pay more
  • Driving Record: Accidents, tickets, and DUIs significantly increase rates
  • Vehicle Type: Sports cars and luxury vehicles cost more to insure
  • Credit Score: Better credit often means lower premiums (in most states)
  • Location: Urban areas with higher accident/theft rates cost more
  • Coverage Level: More coverage = higher premium, but better protection
  • Annual Mileage: More miles driven = higher risk

How to Save Money on Car Insurance

  • Shop Around: Compare quotes from 3-5 companies - rates vary by $500+ for the same coverage
  • Bundle Policies: Combine auto + home/renters insurance for 10-25% discounts
  • Increase Deductibles: Raising from $250 to $1,000 can save 15-30%
  • Ask About Discounts: Good student, safe driver, low mileage, anti-theft, defensive driving course
  • Maintain Good Credit: Improving credit score from fair to good can save $500+/year
  • Review Coverage Annually: As your car ages, dropping collision/comprehensive may make sense
  • Pay in Full: Avoid monthly installment fees ($20-50/year savings)
