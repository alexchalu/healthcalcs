📋 Virginia Auto Insurance Requirements

Virginia requires all drivers to carry minimum liability coverage of 25/50/20 (bodily injury per person / bodily injury per accident / property damage). The average VA driver pays $1,560/year, but rates vary significantly based on your location within the state, driving history, vehicle, and credit score.

Frequently Asked Questions — Virginia Auto Insurance

What is the minimum auto insurance required in Virginia?
Virginia requires minimum liability coverage of 25/50/20 — that's $25k bodily injury per person, $50k per accident, and $20k property damage. Most experts recommend higher limits.
How much does auto insurance cost in Virginia?
The average Virginia driver pays about $1,560 per year ($130/month). Your actual rate depends on age, driving history, vehicle, location, and credit score.
How can I get cheap car insurance in Virginia?
Compare quotes from at least 3-5 insurers, maintain a clean driving record, raise your deductible, bundle with home insurance, and ask about all available discounts. Shopping annually can save hundreds.
Does Virginia use credit scores for auto insurance?
Most Virginia insurers consider credit-based insurance scores when setting rates. Improving your credit score can significantly lower your premium — sometimes by 30-50%.
